About Sri Lanka

Location South Asia, island in the Indian Ocean, southeast of India
Capital Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte
Population ~22 million (as of 2025 estimate)
National flag

Sri Lanka is a beautiful island nation in South Asia, located in the Indian Ocean just below India. Known as the "Pearl of the Indian Ocean," it is famous for its rich culture, stunning beaches, ancient temples, and wildlife. The country's capital is Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte, and Colombo is the largest city and commercial hub. With a warm tropical climate and friendly people, Sri Lanka is a top destination for travelers around the world.

Main Religions in Sri Lanka

Buddhism Majority religion in Sri Lanka (about 70% of the population). Introduced in the 3rd century BCE by Arahat Mahinda from India. Mostly followed by the Sinhalese people. The main tradition is TheravƒÅda Buddhism. Important religious sites: Temple of the Tooth (Dalada Maligawa) - Kandy Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa - ancient Buddhist cities.
Hinduism Practiced by around 12- 15% of the population. Mainly followed by the Sri Lankan Tamils, especially in the Northern and Eastern provinces. Worship of many gods such as Shiva, Vishnu, and Murugan. Major Hindu temples: Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil - Jaffna Munneswaram Temple - Chilaw
Islam About 9-10% of the population are Muslims. Practiced by the Sri Lankan Moors, Malays, and some Indian Muslims. Belief in one God (Allah) and the Prophet Muhammad. Key practices: prayer, fasting during Ramadan, charity, pilgrimage (Hajj). Mosques (Masjids) are spread across the country, like: Red Mosque (Jami Ul-Alfar) - Colombo
Christianity About 7-8% of the population are Christians. Includes Roman Catholics and Protestants (Anglicans, Methodists, etc.). Introduced by Portuguese, Dutch, and British during colonial periods. Belief in Jesus Christ as the Son of God. Famous churches: St. Anthony's Shrine - Kochchikade, Colombo St. Lucia's Cathedral - Colombo
